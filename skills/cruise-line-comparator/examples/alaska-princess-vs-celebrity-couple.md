# Alaska Couple Example

## User Prompt

We're a couple in our late 50s planning a 7-night Alaska cruise. Budget is flexible but we care about itinerary, glacier viewing, good food, and not being on a ship that feels too chaotic. We're comparing Princess and Celebrity. Which is better?

## Expected Output Shape

TOP RECOMMENDATION: Princess if itinerary and glacier access are the top priority

RUNNER-UP: Celebrity if premium ship feel, dining, and adult atmosphere matter more than Alaska-specific depth

AVOID / ONLY IF: Either line on a weaker itinerary with fewer Alaska marquee stops, poor port times, or a ship that does not match the desired pace

Confidence: Medium until exact ships, dates, cabin type, and fare inclusions are provided

Why:
- Princess is a classic Alaska fit when itinerary, port timing, and destination programming are the deciding factors.
- Celebrity can feel more premium onboard, especially for adults who care about dining and design.
- The exact itinerary matters more than the brand: glacier day, port mix, time in port, and pre/post options can flip the answer.

| Criteria | Princess | Celebrity |
|---|---|---|
| Best traveler fit | Destination-focused Alaska couples | Premium-feel adult couples |
| Budget fit | Often competitive; compare Plus/Premier bundles | Can price higher depending on ship and fare type |
| Food and dining | Solid, more classic | Stronger premium dining perception |
| Entertainment and activities | Destination-forward, calmer pace | Adult-focused, polished atmosphere |
| Cabin/value notes | Compare balcony and mini-suite value | Compare included perks and fare type |
| Itinerary fit | Usually very strong for Alaska | Strong when itinerary includes desired glacier/ports |
| Hidden-cost risk | Bundles, excursions, gratuities, Wi-Fi | Fare type, drinks/Wi-Fi, gratuities, excursions |

Fit / not-fit:
- Princess: Best if the Alaska itinerary is the star; not ideal if onboard dining/design is the primary reason to cruise.
- Celebrity: Best if the ship experience matters as much as Alaska; not ideal if the Princess itinerary has meaningfully better glacier or port timing.

Hidden-cost watchouts:
- Alaska excursions can dominate the total budget.
- Compare bundled fares, gratuities, Wi-Fi, drinks, and specialty dining in the final checkout.
- Pre/post hotels, transfers, and flights can change the real value.

Caveats:
- I do not have live fares, ship condition, itinerary deployments, or current promotions here, so treat this as planning guidance and verify final checkout totals.
- Compare the exact ships and sail dates before deciding.
