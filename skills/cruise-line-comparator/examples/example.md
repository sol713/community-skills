# Carnival vs Royal Caribbean Family Example

## User Prompt

We're a family of four with kids ages 9 and 13, looking for a 7-night Caribbean cruise from Florida. Budget is around $4,000 before flights. Our top priorities are waterslides and activities, decent food, and not feeling nickel-and-dimed. We're comparing Carnival and Royal Caribbean. Which is better?

## Expected Output Shape

TOP RECOMMENDATION: Royal Caribbean if the quote fits the budget on a newer or larger activity-focused ship

RUNNER-UP: Carnival if the base fare is meaningfully lower and the family values casual fun over polished production

AVOID / ONLY IF: Older ships from either line if the kids expect big slides, major entertainment, and lots of included activities

| Criteria | Carnival | Royal Caribbean |
|---|---|---|
| Best traveler fit | Value-focused families who like casual energy | Activity-focused families who want bigger ship features |
| Budget fit | Often strong base-fare value | Can be higher, especially on newer ships |
| Food and dining | Casual included food can be a plus | More specialty dining variety, with extra-cost temptation |
| Entertainment and activities | Fun, lively, less formal | Stronger large-ship activity depth |
| Cabin/value notes | Watch ship age and cabin location | Watch ship class and dynamic pricing |
| Itinerary fit | Strong Caribbean coverage | Strong Caribbean and private destination options |
| Hidden-cost risk | Drinks, Wi-Fi, specialty dining, gratuities | Drinks, Wi-Fi, specialty dining, activities, gratuities |

Fit / not-fit:
- Carnival: Best if keeping the fare low matters most; not ideal if the family expects mega-ship attractions.
- Royal Caribbean: Best if activities are the deciding factor; not ideal if the quote forces a cabin or sailing compromise.

Hidden-cost watchouts:
- Gratuities, drink packages, Wi-Fi, arcade or premium activities, specialty dining, shore excursions, and port fees.

Freshness caveat:
Current fares, promos, onboard package prices, and ship deployments change often, so compare final checkout totals for the same dates, cabin type, and itinerary before deciding.
